Overview

Community support plays a vital role in helping individuals access and effectively use assistive technology. Various organizations, online groups, and local initiatives provide essential resources.

Online Support Communities

  • Facebook Groups: Many assistive technology communities exist where users share experiences and resources.
  • Reddit Forums: Subreddits like r/AssistiveTechnology provide discussions on different AT tools.
  • ATIA Online Community: The Assistive Technology Industry Association (ATIA) offers an online space for professionals and users.

Local and National Organizations

  • State AT Programs: Many states have programs that provide loans, training, and demonstrations of AT devices.
  • Centers for Independent Living: These centers help people with disabilities live independently by providing AT services.
  • National Disability Organizations: Groups like the United Spinal Association and the National Federation of the Blind offer resources for AT users.

Workshops and Training

Many organizations provide training sessions for AT users, educators, and caregivers:

  • Webinars: Organizations such as CAST and AbleNet host regular online workshops.
  • Local AT Centers: Many communities have AT resource centers offering free or low-cost training.
  • Schools and Universities: Some institutions offer AT-focused professional development for educators.
