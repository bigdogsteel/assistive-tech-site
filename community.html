Overview

Community support plays a crucial role in helping individuals with disabilities access and effectively use assistive technology. Many organizations, online communities, and advocacy groups provide essential resources.

Support Organizations & Networks

  • Assistive Technology Industry Association (ATIA) - Offers training, research, and industry support for assistive technology.
  • American Association of People with Disabilities (AAPD) - Advocacy and policy support for individuals with disabilities.
  • American Foundation for the Blind - Resources and advocacy for visually impaired individuals.
  • National Organization on Disability (NOD) - Supports disability employment and accessibility initiatives.
  • Understood.org - Support for parents of children with learning disabilities.

Online Support Communities

  • Reddit: Assistive Technology - Community discussions and peer support.
  • Facebook Assistive Technology Groups - Connect with AT users and professionals.
  • e-NABLE - Open-source community providing 3D-printed prosthetics.

Local & State AT Centers

Many states have dedicated assistive technology centers that offer device demonstrations, training, and loan programs. To find a local center, visit:

  • Rehabilitation Engineering and Assistive Technology Society of North America (RESNA) - AT training and certification.
  • AT3 Center - A network of state assistive technology programs.
