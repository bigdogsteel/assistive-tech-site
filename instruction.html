Overview

Using assistive technology effectively in education requires intentional instructional strategies to support diverse learners.

Key Instructional Strategies

  • Universal Design for Learning (UDL): Ensures accessibility by providing multiple means of engagement, representation, and expression.
  • Individualized Education Plans (IEPs): Incorporating assistive technology into IEPs to meet specific student needs.
  • Explicit Instruction: Direct teaching strategies for AT tools, ensuring students understand how to use them effectively.
  • Blended Learning: Combining traditional instruction with digital and assistive technologies.
  • Scaffolding & Differentiation: Adjusting supports based on student progress when using assistive tools.

Implementing Assistive Technology in the Classroom

Steps for successful AT integration:

  • Identify students' needs and match them with appropriate AT tools.
  • Provide hands-on training for students and educators.
  • Monitor progress and adjust tools as needed.
  • Encourage collaboration between educators, students, and families.

Training and Support for Educators

Resources available for educators to learn about AT:

  • Professional Development Workshops: Many school districts offer AT training.
  • Online Courses: Platforms like Coursera and EdX provide AT-related courses.
  • Assistive Technology Specialists: Consulting with specialists to implement best practices.
