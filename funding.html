Overview

Assistive technology can be expensive, but various funding sources exist to help individuals, families, and schools afford the necessary tools and devices.

Government Funding Programs

  • Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA): Provides funding for assistive technology in schools.
  • Medicaid & Medicare: Covers certain AT devices based on medical necessity.
  • Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI): Offers financial assistance for individuals with disabilities.

Grants and Nonprofit Organizations

  • The National Federation of the Blind: Provides grants for assistive technology for visually impaired individuals.
  • United Cerebral Palsy: Offers financial aid for assistive technology devices.
  • Assistive Technology Act Programs: State-run programs that provide funding and device loans.

Crowdfunding and Other Financial Support

  • GoFundMe & DonorsChoose: Crowdfunding platforms where individuals and educators can raise funds.
  • Local Charities and Community Organizations: Many community programs provide financial aid for individuals with disabilities.
  • Employer Assistance Programs: Some workplaces offer funding or reimbursement for assistive tools needed for employees with disabilities.
