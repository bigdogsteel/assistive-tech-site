Overview

Assistive tools and devices help individuals with disabilities perform daily tasks, access education, and enhance their independence. Below are different categories of assistive technology tools.

Types of Assistive Tools

  • Screen Readers: JAWS, NVDA
  • Speech-to-Text: Dragon NaturallySpeaking
  • Text-to-Speech: Read & Write, Apple VoiceOver
  • Adaptive Keyboards & Mice: Logitech Adaptive Mice
  • Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) Devices: Proloquo2Go, Tobii Dynavox
  • Hearing Aids & FM Systems: Oticon Hearing Aids, Phonak FM Systems
  • Braille Displays & Notetakers: HumanWare, Focus Blue Braille Display

Support Organizations & Resources

  • Assistive Technology Industry Association (ATIA) – AT research, training, and conferences.
  • Understood.org – Support for parents of children with learning disabilities.
  • AbleData – Database of AT products and reviews.
  • American Foundation for the Blind – Resources for individuals with visual impairments.
  • American Association of People with Disabilities (AAPD) – Advocacy and resources for disabled individuals.

How to Choose the Right Assistive Tool

When selecting assistive technology, consider factors like the user's needs, ease of use, compatibility with other devices, and affordability. Consulting a specialist or occupational therapist can be beneficial.
