Overview

Assistive tools and devices help individuals with disabilities perform daily tasks, access education, and enhance their independence. Below are different categories of assistive technology tools.

Types of Assistive Tools

  • Screen Readers: Software like JAWS and NVDA that read on-screen text aloud.
  • Speech-to-Text: Tools like Dragon NaturallySpeaking that convert speech into written text.
  • Text-to-Speech: Apps such as Read&Write and Googleâ€™s VoiceOver that read text aloud.
  • Adaptive Keyboards & Mice: Specialized keyboards and trackballs for users with motor impairments.
  • Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) Devices: Devices like Proloquo2Go that assist non-verbal individuals in communication.
  • Hearing Aids & FM Systems: Tools that amplify sound for individuals with hearing impairments.
  • Braille Displays & Notetakers: Devices that translate text into braille for visually impaired users.

How to Choose the Right Assistive Tool

When selecting assistive technology, consider factors like the user's needs, ease of use, compatibility with other devices, and affordability. Consulting a specialist or occupational therapist can be beneficial.
